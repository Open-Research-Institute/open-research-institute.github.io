ORI is the largest open trust network in your world.

When you meet someone who flies under the "ORI" banner, it doesn't tell you anything specific about what they know or believe, or how much you should trust them. What it tells you is that they consent to an intense and fairly invasive protocol for exchanging truth & testing for trust.

A poetic way to think about it is that ORI is trying to be the largest, beautiful, but coherent story that anyone can contribute to.

The ORI story evolves towards coherence (1) across its members and (2) with reality. Coherence does not mean uniformity - it means high bandwidth positive sum interaction.

When I talk about onboarding people to the ORI org, I mean "org" in both senses of the word: an organization, with jobs to do & resources to allocate, and "organism". We regularly test information propagation across the network, the same way a body does to make sure all cells are still responsive and the organism can see exactly what is "part of the organism" and what is not.

- Defender, April 13, 2026

See: "I started writing a fictional autiobiography set in the real world" by Defender, Aug 12, 2024, for Defender's introduction to the ORI story.

This is the communication protocol that allows you to map someone's mind & worldview, even if you're the only one using it. If both use it, then it significantly speeds up calibration & information propagation across the network
    • See A_B_U.html for a concise technical explanation, "How we grade presentation night" for a layperson explanation, and "Why A/B/U is so useful for me" for a personal reflection from Alex.

  • Anatomy of an Internet Argument
    • The purpose of this project is to scale the adjudication of conflict in the court of public opinion. Instead of a million people trying to figure out whether to trust a person, we perform the adjudication in a way that everyone can see and contribute to without duplicating efforts.
    • This also is another vector of onboarding - the propagation of this project transforms hostile, unproductive conversations on the internet to mutually beneficial positive sum ones.
    • See project page on GitHub for all the links.
