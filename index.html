üî≠ Vision üî≠

The Open Research Institute (ORI) is a community & a culture. We believe no institution has a monopoly on the pursuit of truth. We believe in a plurality of paradigms and ways of knowing. Mystic or scientist, the final arbitrer of truth is your ability to predict & manipulate reality.

We believe there is only one reality that we all have access to. Any conflict between those who seek truth can be resolved through understanding of both paradigms & translating between them. The ORI exists to facilitate & mediate this.

We seek to find all those on the frontier of human knowledge, across all domains, empirical & the spiritual, industry & academia, science & art. We want to find our peers, as well as those who disagree with us, and overlay & compare all prespectives.

When someone stumbles on novel, groundbreaking knowledge, whether it comes from an academic paper, a company, or twitter/discord, we want to help it reach people who will use it (peer reviewers, developers, companies, or the world at large).

We need all the help we can get it. Please join us.

~ Defender

üíõ How to Join üíõ

To join the Open Research Institute, all you have to do is publish your research notes somewhere on the internet and tell us about it.

We recommend:

  1. Creating an open research notebook with Obsidian + GitHub Pages
  2. Create a "now" page / research statement about your current work, and what your funding or collaboration needs are
    • Example 1: Chris Lakin is working on human alignment & his statement is the about page of his substack
    • Example 2: DefenderOfBasic is doing an independent, internet PhD in memetics, his statement is a GitHub readme
    • Example 3: Workflowsauce has a "collab with me" google doc with what he's actively working on (education, communication, community)
    • Example 4: Ivan Vendrov maintains a google doc of "shovel ready projects" for solving coordination / alignment of recommender systems
